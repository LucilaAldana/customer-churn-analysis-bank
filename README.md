# Bank Customer Churn Analysis

## by Lucila Aldana Quiñonez | Marketing Data Analyst

📄 Report available in: English - Spanish

This project develops an interactive Tableau dashboard to analyze customer churn behavior for a retail bank operating in France, Germany, and Spain.

The objective was to identify high-risk customer segments, understand the drivers behind churn, and provide actionable retention strategies based on demographic, financial, engagement, and geographic patterns.

The dashboard analyzes:

• Overall churn rate and customer distribution

• Churn by age group and tenure

• Geographic churn comparison (France, Germany, Spain)

• Churn by gender

• Product subscription count and churn relationship

• Complaint behavior and churn correlation

• Account balance segmentation

• Active vs. inactive membership behavior

• Credit score, salary, card type, and points earned impact

Key insights supported by the dashboard include:

• Complaint behavior as the dominant churn driver (nearly universal churn among complaining customers)

• Higher churn concentration in Germany driven by elevated complaint rates

• Increased churn among customers aged 40–55

• Elevated churn among customers holding three or more products

• Identification of inactive zero-balance accounts as an operational inefficiency

The project simulates a real-world churn analysis scenario where customer profiling, behavioral segmentation, and geographic insights are integrated into a centralized dashboard to support retention strategy design and operational improvements.

Data limitations such as the absence of timestamps (onboarding, churn date, complaint date) and lack of complaint reason codes are documented to ensure realistic interpretation of findings.

Tools Used: Tableau / Generative AI / Customer Segmentation / KPI Development / Churn Analysis / Dashboard Design / Business Strategy Insights
